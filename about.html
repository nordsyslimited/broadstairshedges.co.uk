About

Hedges done considered. Locally.

Broadstairs & Hedges is run by Richard Lim. Coast-aware, honest, no upselling.

What I do

I cut hedges. I reduce overgrown ones. I remove the ones that have to go. And I plant new ones — with species chosen for the Thanet coast, not whatever the nursery has in stock.

That is it. I do not do tree surgery, lawn care, fencing or the rest of the garden-services bundle. Hedges are a craft worth being good at, and I keep my focus narrow so I am.

Why local matters here

Much of Broadstairs' Victorian and Edwardian core sits inside one of the town's three conservation areas — Broadstairs CA, St Peter's CA or Reading Street CA — and the section-211 rules apply to any tree over 75mm at chest height. The clifftop belt from Stone Bay up to Kingsgate sits above the Thanet Coast SSSI and the Thanet Coast & Sandwich Bay SPA/Ramsar site, so cliff-edge and roof-adjacent work in nesting season needs a survey before anything starts. The Margate Chalk under the topsoil is thin, marl-free, and sharply alkaline — beech and Leylandii struggle where escallonia and Olearia thrive. Manston records 1,846 hours of sunshine a year but only 613mm of rain; new plantings die from drought here more than from anything else.

A contractor without that patch-specific knowledge will not know any of it without being told. I work this coast every week. The conservation-area paperwork is included. The herring-gull nesting protocol is built into how I schedule cliff-adjacent work. The species recommendations match what actually lives here.

How I work

  • Photos and postcode for an initial quote. I can give a fixed price for most routine cuts without coming out. Reductions and removals usually need a site visit (free, no obligation).
  • Fixed-price quotes. No day-rate creep. If the job runs longer than I expected, that is my problem, not yours.
  • Conservation-area paperwork handled. If your address needs a section-211 notice to Thanet District Council, I file it. Cost included in the quote.
  • Nesting-aware timing on cliff-adjacent work. A quick survey before any Mar–Sep work within reach of the SSSI edge, and honest advice on what should wait for October.
  • All arisings cleared. Clippings go to Margate HWRC (Manston Road CT9 4LX) or get chipped, never burned on site.
  • Bank transfer or cash. Invoice or receipt either way.

What you will not get

  • A quiet cliff-edge job in June that ignores the nesting law.
