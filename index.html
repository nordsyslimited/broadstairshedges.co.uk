Why local matters

A Broadstairs hedge is not a generic hedge.

The Thanet coast is the type area for the Margate Chalk Member of the Upper Cretaceous — thin, sharply alkaline topsoil, marl-free and flint-poor. Manston records 1,846 hours of sunshine a year (1991–2020), which puts Broadstairs among the sunniest places in the UK, with rainfall of just 613mm. Prevailing wind is westerly and south-westerly, but the killer vector for coastal planting is the N/NE salt-laden air off the North Sea — that decides which species live and which die.

I work to that reality, not against it. Escallonia and Olearia on the seafront where Griselinia is too tender in a hard East Kent winter. Yew and hawthorn set back where beech would scorch through July. Native hawthorn on chalk-loving field boundaries out towards Reading Street. And a tape measure on any stem approaching the 75mm conservation-area threshold before I touch it.

Inside a conservation area?

I handle the paperwork.

Broadstairs has three designated conservation areas: Broadstairs CA (extended twice, most recently 23 July 2009), St Peter's CA and Reading Street CA (both 13 April 1973). Any tree with a stem over 75mm at chest height needs six weeks' written notice to Thanet District Council before pruning. I file the section-211 notice on your behalf, included in every quote.
