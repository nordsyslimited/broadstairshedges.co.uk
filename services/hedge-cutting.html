Service · Hedge cutting

Routine cuts done properly, on every species we work with.

The bread-and-butter job: annual or twice-yearly cuts on hedges that are already at a good height. Done right, this is what keeps a hedge looking like it belongs to the house.

What's included

  • Top, sides and front face cut to your chosen height and width
  • All arisings cleared from site and disposed of properly (we carry an EA upper-tier waste-carrier registration)
  • Brush-down of the path, lawn or driveway underneath
  • Six-week section-211 notice filed on your behalf if you're in a conservation area and the hedge has any stems over 75mm at chest height
  • Fixed price quoted in advance — no day-rate creep

Pricing

Most routine cuts in Canterbury fall in the £120 – £400 range depending on hedge length, height, and how much access we need. We give a fixed price after seeing photos and the postcode — no surprises on the day, no charge if the quote isn't accepted.

Timing by species

The best window depends on what you're growing.

  • Privet, Leylandii, thuja — fast-growing, often needs two cuts a year (late spring and late summer)
  • Beech, hornbeam — single late-summer cut (August to early September) keeps the russet winter leaves
  • Yew — single late-August cut; yew is happy with a hard clip once a year and resentful of more
  • Holly, laurel — late summer; both bruise easily, so we prefer secateurs and loppers on visible faces over a hedge trimmer where time allows
  • Native mixed (hawthorn, blackthorn, hazel) — winter only (September to February) to comply with nesting law

What we don't do on cutting jobs

If a hedge needs more than a routine cut — it's overgrown, gappy, leaning, or has outgrown the position — that's a reduction job, not a cut. We'll tell you that at quote stage rather than do a botched cut and pretend it solved the problem.
