Tree services · Crown raising

Raising the crown — clearance without wrecking the outline.

Crown raising (also called crown elevation) removes the lower branches of a tree to give clearance underneath. Pedestrians can walk under it, vehicles can pass, the building next to it stops taking scrapes on the render, or the sightline down towards the seafront opens up. Done right, the tree above still looks like a tree.

When raising is the right call

  • A mature front-garden lime or sycamore on Gladstone Road, Rectory Road or Norfolk Road whose lower branches are dragging on the pavement and forcing walkers into the road
  • Vehicle-height clearance over a driveway — the standard target is 2.5m for a car, 4.5m for a delivery van, 5.2m for a road
  • Sightlines at a junction or at the top of a drive where a tree has grown into the line of view — an issue on some of the narrower Broadstairs cliff-top approaches
  • A tree leaning on the render of a house or garage — lower limbs off, then re-check whether a reduction is also needed higher up

How high to raise

A rule of thumb: after raising, the live crown should still be at least two-thirds of the total tree height. Take too much off the bottom and the tree looks bare-legged; it's also mechanically weaker in a wind because the leverage on the remaining branches has increased. On a clifftop or exposed seafront plot that leverage penalty matters more than it does inland. If the maths says the tree needs to lose more than a third of its live crown for the clearance you want, a raise is the wrong tool — you probably need a reduction, a fell, or to change the target height.

Species notes

  • Lime, sycamore, London plane — the classic raise candidates. All three respond well and grow into a good shape above.
  • Holm oak — raises cleanly on the seafront plots; keep the timing to late winter or midsummer, avoid the spring flush.
  • Oak (English/sessile) — light raise only, and only outside spring flush. Oaks resent heavy lower-limb work.
  • Cedar, monkey puzzle, ornamental conifers — a raise fundamentally changes their form. Owners often regret it. I'll flag it before I cut.
  • Fruit trees (apple, pear) — often raise-and-thin together for orchard picking access.

Pricing

Single-tree crown raise in Broadstairs typically runs £220 – £600. Roadside or over-driveway work sometimes needs a small MEWP (cherry-picker) hire for one day and coning of the road, which adds cost; I call this out on the quote rather than absorb it into a vague day rate. Narrow-access Broadstairs terraces occasionally need bag-out rather than chipping, which adds a small labour line — again, called out up front.

Before I cut

Same as all the tree work — Thanet District Council conservation-area and TPO check first. Broadstairs, St Peter's and Reading Street CAs cover most of the Victorian and Edwardian core. If the tree is protected, that changes the paperwork window; I do that check as part of the quote.
