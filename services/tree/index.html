Services · Tree work

Tree services in Broadstairs — five standard operations, done properly.

The mature trees behind your hedge need different work at different points in their life. I do the five that come up on almost every Broadstairs and St Peter's garden: pruning, crown thinning, crown raising, crown reduction and deadwooding. Clifftop wind-load, ash dieback and TPO / conservation-area paperwork all handled as part of the quote.

Pruning

Selective removal of dead, diseased or structurally unsound parts — branches, buds, roots. The general tree work every mature garden tree needs on a cycle. Formative pruning on young trees; maintenance pruning on the established sycamores, limes and holm oaks that fill the older Broadstairs gardens.

Pruning detail & pricing

Crown thinning

Selective removal of live branches to reduce canopy density. Wind passes through instead of loading the tree as a solid sail — which matters more on the Thanet clifftop than almost anywhere else in Kent. First choice on dense mature sycamores above Stone Bay and Kingsgate.

How thinning works

Crown raising

Also called crown elevation. Removing lower branches for pedestrian, vehicle or building clearance, or to open a sightline down towards the seafront. The usual answer when a mature front-garden tree on Gladstone Road or Rectory Road has been under-managed for a decade.

When to raise

Crown reduction

Reducing the overall height or spread by cutting branches back to laterals of a sensible size. Not topping — that's the wrong approach and I don't do it. Reduction protects the tree's future shape while dropping the wind-loading and the neighbour-boundary pressure now.

Reduction vs topping

Deadwooding

Removal of dead, dying or diseased branches from the canopy. Purely for safety and tree health — nothing structural. Standard annual maintenance on established garden trees, especially over paths, driveways and the flat roofs common on Broadstairs terraces.

Before I cut

TPO and conservation-area checks — included.

Broadstairs has three designated conservation areas — Broadstairs CA, St Peter's CA and Reading Street CA — covering much of the Victorian and Edwardian core. Any tree over 75mm in diameter at 1.5m needs six weeks' section-211 notice to Thanet District Council before work. A tree with a Tree Preservation Order needs a full application with 8 weeks' notice. I check both, file the paperwork, and quote knowing which window we're working in — no last-minute stop-work order on the day. Unauthorised work on a protected tree can carry a fine of up to £20,000 per tree, so the paperwork is not optional.
